Conclusion Auto-Encoders and Centroid Encoders

  1. Dashboard
Styled Page

In our group project, we sought to understand the theory and practical applications for Auto-Encoders and Centroid Encoders (CE) .Auto-Encoders (AE) are specialized forms of artificial neural networks designed primarily for unsupervised learning tasks. They function by processing unlabeled data through a specific architecture that compresses the input into a lower-dimensional latent space, often referred to as the bottleneck, before reconstructing the output. This compression and subsequent reconstruction enable autoencoders to focus on capturing the most important features of the data. Autoencoders work by encoding the high-dimensional data into a lower-dimensional space and then decoding it back to the original dimension.

Centroid encoders is specifically designed for data visualization when class labels are available. Centroid encoders is capable of managing both really large datasets, such as the SUSY analysis containing over a million data points, and smaller, high-dimensional biological datasets. What makes centroid encoders particularly interesting is its adaptability and its capacity to maintain clear class separability through supervised learning, showing that it has an advantage over traditional methods like PCA. These older methods often struggle with overlapping classes in complex datasets, making centroid encoders's precision in class distinction very useful.

During our review, we explored several significant benefits of centroid encoders and autoencoders, including its efficiency and the innovative features and understanding the application of these dimension reduction processes. Moreover, we were intrigued by the potential of extending centroid encoders into unsupervised and semi-supervised learning. Such extensions could be crucial for applications where complete labeled datasets are sparse or unavailable. Our discussions highlighted how centroid encoders improves traditional autoencoder frameworks by integrating supervised learning to optimize data visualization effectively. Our project highlighted key differences and similarities from methods we learned in our Big Data course and in the Supervised Dimensionality Reduction and Visualization using Centroid-Encoder paper.
